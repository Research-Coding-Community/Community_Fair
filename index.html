Research Coding Community Fair

Overview

The Research Coding Community Fair brought together stand holders, speakers, demonstrators, and visitors from research communities and technical teams across the University. The aim was to make support, training, tools, and community routes more visible in one shared room.

The format was deliberately informal. Visitors could arrive when convenient, move between stands, join the speaker hour, and use the People Bingo activity to start useful conversations. Across the afternoon there were 6 stands, 7 speakers, and around 90 people signed up to attend throughout the event.

The event was supported by Bioinform@exe via ELIXIR-UK.

Review and Lessons Learnt

This section will hold a fuller reflection on the Community Fair: what we set out to test, how the format worked in practice, and what we learnt from bringing research software, digital research communities, training routes, and technical initiatives into one shared space.

The final version should capture the practical experience of running the event, including what helped visitors engage with the stands, where the schedule supported useful conversations, and where future fairs could be made easier to organise, navigate, and follow up.

It should also record the lessons that are useful beyond this single afternoon: how to make community activity visible, how to lower the barrier to asking technical questions, how to connect people to the right support, and how to turn an informal event into lasting routes for collaboration.
